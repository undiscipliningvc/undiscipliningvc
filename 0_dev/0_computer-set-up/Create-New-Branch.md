# Create a New Branch

## Steps to Create a New Branch

1. Open Visual Studio Code and be sure you are in the UVC repo.

2. Look in the lower left-hand corner and ensure that you are on the dev branch. Hit the sync circle to ensure you have everything pulled down.

3. Click on "dev" in the lower left-hand-corner.

4. A new dropdown will appear at the top middle of the screen.

5. From that dropdown, select the option that says, "Create new branch" (first option).

6. Enter the name for your branch (e.g., dev-adrian) and hit enter.

Note: You can give it any name you want, but I recommend using lower case, hypens instead of spaces, and the dev-name model, as in the example above, to avoid any confusion.

7. Click the "Source Control" icon on the left side of the screen. It's three connected circles that branch off from each other.

8. On the left you will see a big blue button that says "Publish Branch". Click it.

9. On the far right a pop up will appear that says "Would you like to create a pull request ...". Hit the X all the way on the right of that to close it, i.e., do not hit the blue button in the pop up. You don't want to do this.

That's it. The new branch exists now and others can get it on their computers to use it.


## For Collaborators: Get a Copy of the New Branch on Your Computer

1. Open Visual Studio Code and be sure you are in the UVC repo, ideally on the "dev" branch. Hit the sync circle to ensure you have everything pulled down.

2. Click the "Source Control" icon on the left side of the screen. It's three connected circles that branch off from each other.

3. Once the "Source Control" pane is open on the left, you will see that it says "Source Control" at the top of the pane, and right below it "Changes". On the "Changes" line, go all the way to the right and you will see three dots like this: ... Click on those. Note that the "Source Control" line also has three dots on the right. Ignore those.

4. Once you click the three dots to the right of "Changes", you will get a dropdown with a bunch of options. Click "Fetch". You will now have the new branch on your computer.

5. Click on the branch name in the lower-left hand corner (e.g., dev). Look for the name of the new branch preceded by the word "origin/" (e.g., origin/dev-adrian) and click it. This automatically creates a local version on your computer and switches you to it. From now on, always ensure the lower-left corner shows the name without "origin" so you know you are working on your local copy.
